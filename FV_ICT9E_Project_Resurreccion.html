Fire Prevention at Home

Install Smoke Detectors
 Install smoke detectors in every level of your house outside of sleeping areas and test them every month to ensure they are still able to function.
Have a Fire Escape Plan
 Having a fire escape plan ready can be handy in times where you only have a few minutes to escape from a burning building since you already know where to go.
Keep firewood and Piles of Dry Leaves Away From Home
 Keeping firewood and large piles of dead leaves near your house can increase its chances of catching on fire in case a grass fire starts. The dry leaves can also help spread the fire if it is blown in multiple directions.
Extinguish Flammable Materials Properly
 Extinguish flammables inside your house such as candles, wood, matches, etc. properly to make sure other flammables like paper or even your curtains don't get caught in it and start a fire.
